Thursday

Named after Thor, the Norse god of thunder. In Latin languages, it's named after Jupiter (Giovedi in Italian, Jeudi in French). Thor's day was traditionally a day of feasting.

Friday

Named after Frigg or Freya, Norse goddesses. In Latin languages, it's named after Venus (Venerdi in Italian, Vendredi in French). The term "TGIF" celebrates the coming weekend.

Saturday

The only day named after a Roman god (Saturn). In Japanese, it's "doy≈çbi" meaning "day of the earth". Traditionally the Jewish Sabbath and a day of rest in many cultures.

Sunday

Named after the Sun. Considered the first day of the week in many cultures and the last in others. Traditionally a day of rest and worship in Christian cultures.
